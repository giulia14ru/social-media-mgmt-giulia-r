Lucky Stash — Premium Botanical Tequila

Strategy

Lucky Stash's social media strategy is centered on differentiation, positioning the brand as a premium and unconventional tequila for individuals who seek distinct experiences and reject the ordinary. The narrative is built around the intersection of heritage and innovation, where the botanical expression becomes the core differentiator, while Blanco and Extra Añejo reinforce the brand's depth and legacy.

Content direction follows a highly editorial approach, inspired by luxury, perfumery, and art — emphasizing sensory storytelling, composition, and visual refinement. Rather than mass appeal, the strategy intentionally targets a niche audience with a mindset-driven identity — individuals drawn to experimentation, creativity, and elevated consumption rituals.

Content Creation with AI
  • Used AI to generate 20+ visual moodboards inspired by luxury perfumery and botanical art
  • Curated the Instagram feed as a visual archive — each post designed as a standalone editorial piece
  • Developed flavor-specific color palettes (botanical green, amber, deep gold) for visual consistency
  • AI-assisted copywriting for sensory descriptors — translating taste into visual language
Platform Adaptation
  • Instagram: Operates as a curated visual archive, showcasing brand aesthetics, botanical compositions, and storytelling. Each post functions as an editorial piece — not an ad.
  • TikTok: Focuses on narrative-driven content and behind-the-scenes processes to humanize the brand — from agave sourcing to botanical infusion techniques.
Social SEO
Keywords woven into captions and text overlays: botanical tequila, premium spirits, craft distillation, sensory experience, elevated drinking, artisanal tequila, mixology ritual
Visual Work Samples
Curated Instagram feed — editorial aesthetic
Botanical composition — sensory storytelling
Behind-the-scenes — distillation process
Performance Focus
  • Creating desire through visual refinement, not mass appeal
  • Increasing saves and profile visits as intent signals
  • Strengthening brand recall through consistent editorial identity
  • Positioning Lucky Stash not just as a product, but as a statement of identity and taste
Business Outcome: DMs shifted from price questions to purchase intent — qualifying intent improved significantly. Stronger purchase intent signals correlated with actual sales lift. Brand positioned as a lifestyle statement, not a commodity.
Key Learning: In premium spirits, saves are the most underrated metric. They indicate intent more honestly than likes. We optimized content specifically for save behavior — and it worked. The editorial approach filtered out casual browsers and attracted the right audience.

Thirstday — Cultural Identity & Emotional Connection

Strategy

Thirstday's social media strategy is built around cultural identity and emotional connection, specifically targeting Mexican and Latino audiences in the U.S. Rather than relying on transient wellness trends, the brand positions itself through a reinterpretation of Mexican heritage — nostalgia, food, sobremesa, and shared social rituals — translated into a modern, visually compelling language.

Content is designed to feel both familiar and elevated, blending playful indulgence with strong cultural references. Each product flavor is developed as a distinct personality, enabling scalable storytelling and audience segmentation across platforms.

Content Creation with AI
  • Used AI to develop flavor personalities — each SKU with distinct voice, color palette, and content archetype
  • Curated the Instagram feed to reflect cultural nostalgia — warm tones, family moments, food-centric visuals
  • AI-generated cultural reference libraries — from Loteria cards to vintage Mexican advertising
  • Created shareable meme formats that resonate with bicultural millennial identity
Platform Adaptation
  • Instagram: Focuses on aesthetic storytelling and curated identity — warm, nostalgic visuals that feel like flipping through a family photo album. Heavy use of carousels for storytelling depth.
  • TikTok: Leans into cultural humor and relatable formats — "things that just make sense," "POV: you're at a Mexican family gathering," and flavor personality skits.
Social SEO
Keywords: mexican heritage, nostalgia, sobremesa, latino culture, bicultural identity, family rituals, food and drink, millennial nostalgia
Visual Work Samples

Performance Focus
  • Prioritizing shareability — content designed to be sent in group chats and family threads
  • Building relatability through cultural specificity, not generic appeal
  • Creating cultural resonance that converts through identity, not novelty
  • Using a mix of lifestyle content, humor, and sensory visuals to drive engagement and brand affinity
Business Outcome: Comment sentiment shifted from product questions to brand affinity and cultural identification. Higher brand recall and shareability. Social data directly informed geographic expansion and new flavor development decisions.
Key Learning: Millennial and Latino audiences detect inauthentic cultural references instantly. We only participated in trends and references where the brand voice had something genuine to add. Authenticity over virality. Identity over novelty.

Reels concepts, before/after optimization, and format innovation.

Reels / Short-form Concepts
Concept 1 — Educational Hook
Hook (0–3s): "Stop stretching your hamstrings. You're doing it wrong."

Script: 15-second educational punch with visual demonstration of correct vs. incorrect form

Visual: Split screen — wrong vs. right form, text overlay with key points

CTA: "Save this for your next leg day"
Concept 2 — Trend Adaptation
Trend: "Things that just make sense"

Brand twist: "Things that just make sense — wellness edition"

Execution: Quick cuts of studio rituals, client moments, instructor energy. Native editing with trending sound.

Why it works: Participates in culture without copying. Brand voice adds genuine value to the trend.
Concept 3 — UGC-Style Authenticity
Format: "POV: You finally found a yoga studio that..."

Visual: Client-filmed footage, minimal polish, maximum authenticity. No studio lighting, no script.

Why it works: Audiences trust "people like me" over brand content. UGC-style outperforms polished brand video by 3x.

Community Management

Turning comments and DMs into relationship-building opportunities.

Tone Principles

  • Respond, don't just reply — add value, not just acknowledgment
  • Match energy — professional for B2B, playful for millennials, warm for wellness
  • Move public to private only when necessary — most resolution happens in comments (social proof)

Example Responses

User comment:
"Love this!"
Response
"Thanks! Which tip are you trying first?" — Extends conversation, boosts comment thread
User question:
"Do you offer online classes?"
Response
"Yes, we have a digital membership. Sending you a DM with details right now." — Immediate action + moves to conversion channel
Crisis Management

Real-Style Scenario

Negative Comment
"I was charged for a class I couldn't attend and never got a response."
Response Strategy
"I'm truly sorry about that experience — it's not the standard we hold ourselves to. Please send us a DM with your name and class date so we can resolve this today. Thank you for letting us know."
Principles applied:
  • Acknowledge publicly (transparency)
  • Empathize without over-apologizing
  • Specific action + timeline
  • Move to private for details
  • Never defensive
